The Fragility of Centralization

Current cybersecurity relies on centralized authorities (DNS registrars, hosting providers) to take down malicious infrastructure.


Vulnerabilities

  • Single Point of Failure: Easy to target or coerce.
  • Censorship: Valid security research can be blocked.
  • Jurisdiction Issues: Legal orders vary by country.

Centralized Takedown

The Solution: DTIP on Ethereum

Moving Threat Intelligence to the Blockchain creates a system that is:

Immutable

Data cannot be retrospectively altered to cover tracks.

Censorship Resistant

No single entity can suppress the data.

Global Availability

Permanent uptime for defensive systems.

System Objectives

Eliminate SPoF

Distribute data so it cannot be taken down by targeting one node.

Data Integrity

Record hashes of reports to ensure history is never manipulated.

Democratize

Allow small orgs access to high-fidelity intel usually reserved for enterprises.

Incentivize

Solve the "free-rider" problem with "Contribute-to-Earn".

Privacy

Share attack signatures via pseudonymous addresses.

System Use Cases

The primary actors are the Administrator (Governance), Researcher (Contributor), and Consumer (User).

Phase 1: Researcher Onboarding

Includes off-chain vetting by admin, on-chain role granting, and mandatory token staking.

Phase 2: Data Submission

Researchers sign data off-chain to save gas. The contract verifies the signature via ecrecover before storing.

Phase 3: Public Query

Consumers calculate the report hash locally and query the contract. This is a read-only (gasless) operation.

Tokenomics: The Carrot & The Stick

The Carrot (Incentives)

  • Submission Rewards: Baseline for new IOCs.
  • Validation Rewards: For verifying others' work.
  • Effectiveness Bonus: Released when Consumers confirm the block was useful.

The Stick (Penalties)

  • Staking: Researchers must lock tokens to join.
  • Slashing: If false data is submitted, the DAO votes to burn the staked tokens.

Expected Outcomes

Lower MTTD & MTTR

Real-time ledger access drastically reduces Mean Time to Detect and Respond.

Reduced Noise

Reputation-based vetting reduces "alert fatigue" and false positives compared to automated scraping.

Source of Truth

A historical, immutable archive for long-term forensic analysis.

Researcher Economy

Direct monetization of expertise attracts more talent to the field.

Future Roadmap

  • DAO Governance: Remove the single Admin. Move to community voting.
  • Oracles: Automate data ingestion from external sources.
  • Cross-Chain: Expand beyond Ethereum for scalability.
